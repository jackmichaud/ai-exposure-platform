# Running and Interpreting a Debate

Guide for using the Agent Debate Arena and understanding its output. For technical implementation, see [../agents/debate-protocol.md](../agents/debate-protocol.md) and [../agents/prompt-templates.md](../agents/prompt-templates.md).

## Picking a Good Debate Topic

The most interesting debates come from **contested occupations** — those with `netDisplacement` between -30 and +30. These have genuine tension between automation risk and augmentation potential.

| Occupation Type | Net Displacement | Debate Quality |
|----------------|------------------|----------------|
| Displacement-dominant (> +30) | High auto, low aug | Predictable — personas mostly agree on risk |
| **Contested (-30 to +30)** | **Balanced** | **Best debates — genuine disagreement** |
| Augmentation-dominant (< -30) | Low auto, high aug | Predictable — personas mostly agree on benefit |

Good starter debates: Financial Analyst, Accountant, IT Support Specialist, Quality Control Inspector.

## What to Watch in Each Round

### Round 1 — Opening Statements
Each persona stakes out their position independently. Look for:
- Does each persona reference specific tasks (not generic AI claims)?
- Are positions distinct from each other?
- Does The Realist identify the most contested tasks?

### Round 2 — Rebuttals
The strongest arguments surface here. Look for:
- Direct engagement with others' points (not talking past each other)
- The Skeptic and Optimist finding specific disagreements
- The Worker Advocate grounding abstract arguments in practical impact

### Round 3 — Closing Statements
Positions may converge or harden. Look for:
- Has anyone shifted their stance based on the debate?
- Are recommendations specific and actionable?
- Does The Realist synthesize the strongest arguments from each side?

## Interpreting the Synthesis

The synthesis is generated by a neutral analyst (not a persona). It produces 6 sections:

1. **Key Takeaways**: The 3-5 most important conclusions. If these feel generic, the debate prompts may need more occupation-specific context.
2. **Risk Assessment**: Calibrated as low/moderate/high/critical. Compare against the occupation's `netDisplacement` — they should roughly align.
3. **Recommendations for Workers**: Should be specific to the occupation, not generic "learn to code" advice.
4. **Projected Changes**: Skills, wages, employment outlook. Check against the occupation's `wageEffect` and `timeline` for consistency.
5. **Areas of Agreement**: Where all 4 personas converge — these are high-confidence conclusions.
6. **Areas of Disagreement**: Where personas diverge — these map to genuine uncertainty in the field.

## Common Failure Modes

| Problem | Symptom | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Generic responses | Personas say "AI will change things" without specifics | Add more task-level detail to the `{tasks}` variable |
| Personas agree too much | Debate feels flat, no tension | Pick a more contested occupation (net displacement closer to 0) |
| Off-topic drift | Personas discuss AI in general, not this occupation | Strengthen the system prompt's "reference specific tasks" rule |
| Synthesis is vague | Key takeaways lack specificity | Ensure Round 3 includes concrete recommendations |
| Response too long/short | Outside 200-400 word target | Adjust `max_tokens` parameter (512-1024 per turn) |

## Cost Estimate

A full 4-persona, 3-round debate + synthesis uses approximately:
- **Input tokens**: 2,000-5,000 (grows with debate history)
- **Output tokens**: ~9,600 (16 turns × 512 avg + 1,024 synthesis)
